Good to know

Using the stamp maker in the UAE, explained

If it's your first time, the process can feel unclear. Here's everything worth knowing before you order — written from what we actually get asked every day.

Why your business needs one

In the UAE, a company stamp is more than a formality. Banks ask for it when you open a corporate account, and it's commonly required on invoices, contracts, tenancy agreements, tender submissions, and letters to government departments. The stamp signals that a document genuinely comes from your company — which is why most official paperwork still expects one alongside a signature.

What to put on your stamp

Most company stamps include the company name exactly as it appears on the trade licence, and often the licence number. Many businesses also add their logo, a second language line (Arabic and English), or a short tagline. Our maker keeps the text crisp and readable at real stamp size — if a line is too long, switching to an oval or rectangle shape usually solves it.

Documents you'll need

To make sure the details on your stamp match your registered company, we ask for a copy of your trade licence (or trade name reservation certificate) and a valid Emirates ID. You upload these securely during checkout — it takes a few seconds and protects you from typos on official paperwork.

Choosing self-inking

Almost every business today chooses a self-inking stamp. The ink pad is built in, so you get a clean impression with a single press — no separate pad, no mess. A good mechanism lasts for thousands of impressions and can be re-inked, which is why we use trusted manufacturer-grade parts rather than cheap alternatives that smudge after a few months.
